Predicting wildfires

INTRODUCTION

Forest fires are one of the major reasons for loss of forest ecosystems and human communities. More than 3 million forest land is destroyed every year. In the longer term, they can adversely affect the supply of environmental services, threaten the survival of endangered species, the composition and structure of forests and the soil quality. Forest fires can be caused due to natural or human activities but whatever might be the cause they need to be managed properly to minimise the damage to forest.

UNDERSTANDING THE PROBLEM

Dealing with forest fire remains a reflex action,not a proactive and thought out approach. Current techniques and dealing methods used by forest rangers and fire fighters such as soil throwing, water spraying, dropping of ammonia from helicopters, cutting down patch of forests to prevent further spread are not efficient enough to deal with forest fires as there is definite way to predict the behaviour of the forest fire which includes speed and direction of fire and possible damage perimeter.Hence forest fires take too long to be controlled due to which significant amount of forest may be affected along with damage to soil and ecosystem which include local flora and fauna.

OUR SOLUTION

We Propose an innovative solution by providing a prediction tool of forest fires using simulation. By using the simulation software we plan to simulate how actually a forest fire propagates and how it varies according to different factors that affect its growth like wind speed, temperature, humidity, etc in a totally virtual environment and from this we can get key data like how fast and how wide the forest fire would spread. This simulation can be changed according to the topological factors of a forest that it is currently simulating can also be used to specify a particular situation.

IMPACT OF OUR SOLUTION

Our forest fire simulator will provide the probable coordinates and perimeter of damage so that local officials can plan and strategize beforehand which areas to concentrate their efforts and more efficiently utilize the available resources to minimize and control the forest fire damage.

CONSTRAINTS TO OVERCOME

We currently need actual data on forest fire, how forest fires behaved in the past so that we can verify it with our simulator or even use it as training data.

FUTURE GOALS

We plan to improvise the simulation by using machine learning methods by using previous forest fires statistical data, which will improve the accuracy of the simulation. We plan to improve the simulation by Integrating a real-time update system into the simulation where the initial simulation prepared by us would be updated according to the data that we get from satellite images and Drone data.
